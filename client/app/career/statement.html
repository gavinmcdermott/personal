Why I Create

"We would know what they thought when they did it."
- Richard Hamming

I've been a founding engineer, grown development teams, and written software that processed billions in transactions. I'm not an engineer.

I've crafted products used by milions and lead design workshops in foreign countries. I'm not a designer.

I exist at the intersection of intuition and application.

And I'd always had a curiosity relating to my tools of choice; so I did a historical dive. And I came to realize, however late in my career it was, something important. I realized that we're all awash in a medium that forgot its own history -- and I have a sense that something important is being lost.

Regaining it will take more than algorithms and advertising.

So it's time for a new path. And it will take a little looking backward [1] to go forward. It will also involve some Wayne Gretzky, lots of showing by doing, and synthesis paired with original thought.

All of this, driven by a deep intent to contribute to, and build upon, the work of giants.

It is still the beginning of our technology-augmented world; infancy [2]. And someone once told me that we can use this magic media, a little lost knowledge, and things not yet known, to think the unthinkable. To create a world we could only once imagine.

This is why I create.

[1] Papers like this showed me how much was missing from the spectrum of general dialogue in the field of computing.

[2] Humans tend to think within a very limited time horizon. In reality, the computer-augmented environment is a very new phenomenon.
