Why I Create

"We would know what they thought when they did it." [1]
- Richard Hamming

[1] I'm often wowed by the magnitude of some folks.

I've been a founding engineer, grown development teams, and written software that processed billions of transactions. I'm not an engineer.

I've crafted products used by milions and lead design workshops in foreign countries. I'm not a designer.

I exist at the intersection of theory, intuition, and application.

And I have a sense that something important is lost -- we're all awash in a medium that forgot its own history.

So it's time to create a new path. And it will take some looking backward to go forward [2].

It will also involve some Wayne Gretzky, lots of showing by doing, and synthesis paired with original thought.

All of this, driven by a beautiful intent to contribute to, and build upon, the work of giants.

[2] Originally it was papers like this which showed me how much is missing from the dialogue in this field.

It is still the beginning of our technology-augmented world; infancy [3].

And someone once told me that we can use this magic media, a little lost knowledge, and things not yet known, to think the unthinkable.

This is why I create.

[3] Humans tend to think within a very limited time horizon. In reality, the computer-augmented environment is a very new phenomenon.
